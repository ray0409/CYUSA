History of computer

History of Computers 19th Century Beginnings The history of computers dates back to the early 19th century when mechanical calculating machines were first theorized and built. In 1801, Joseph Marie Jacquard invented a loom that utilized punched cards for weaving fabric designs, laying the groundwork for future computing devices. Charles Babbage conceived the Difference Engine in 1821, a steam-driven machine intended to compute numerical tables, although it was never completed due to technological limitations. The late 19th century saw Herman Hollerith develop a punch-card system for the 1890 U.S. Census, which greatly expedited data processing and led to the formation of IBM.
